Nigerian Egusi Soup Recipe



This is a rich melon seed soup, often eaten with pounded yam, fufu, or eba


Servings: 6

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 40 minutes

Difficulty: Beginner


Ingredients


  • 2 cups of egusi (melon seeds)
  • 500g assorted meats (goat meat, beef, shaki/tripe, cow foot)
  • 200g stockfish - soaked in warm water
  • 1 cup dried fish (washed and deboned)
  • 2-3 cups fresh palm oil
  • 1 medium onions
  • 3 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2 scrotch bonnet (atarodo) - ground or blended
  • Boiled meat stock
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste

Tip:
For a good flavour and texture, roast the egusi seeds on a fry pan for 2-3 mins
before grounding. This will give it a light,
nutty aroma and deepens the flavour of the egusi soup

Instructions


  1. Boil the meat, Season meat and stockfish with salt,
    seasoning cubes, and onions. Boil until tender,
    keeping the stock for later
  2. Make Egusi Paste in a bowl, mix ground egusi with
    a little water to form a thick paste
  3. Heat palm oil in a large pot (medium heat). add chopped onions
    and stir for 1-2 mins
  4. Add the egusi paste and fry for 8-10 mins, stir continously
    until it starts to form little lumps
  5. Pour in the meat stock, and add ground crayfish and
    blended pepper, and stir well, cook for another 10 mins
  6. Add meat, dried fish, and more stock if needed. Simmer for 5
    mins so the flavours can blend
  7. Add chopped spinach/pumpkin leaves, stir, and cook for 3-5 mins
  8. Adjust seasoning and serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or eba
